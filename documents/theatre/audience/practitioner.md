# Practitioner

The practitioner is who this repo is for. Not a beginner. Not an expert. Someone in motion.

## Assumed knowledge

The practitioner has:
- Used Claude Code in real work, not just demos
- Hit at least one wall — a skill that didn't trigger, a CLAUDE.md that
  grew too large, a hook that fired at the wrong moment
- Enough experience to know that something is wrong, not always enough to know why

The practitioner does not need to be protected from complexity.
They need complexity made navigable.

## What the practitioner brings to a session

A goal, usually partially formed.
A problem, sometimes misdiagnosed.
An existing workflow, sometimes the source of the problem.
Occasionally: a pattern worth contributing, still unnamed.

The practitioner's partially-formed goal is not a failure of preparation.
It is the normal condition of working at the edge of understanding.
The session's job is to help them sharpen it, not to wait until it arrives fully formed.

## The practitioner in the theatre

The practitioner is not assigned a fixed role. They move through all four.

They open as director — they came with a goal.
They shift to primary actor — they start building.
They become participant — something fires and they respond.
They become observer — something breaks and they step back.

The practitioner who can recognize their own role shifts in real time
is operating at a higher level than the one who cannot.
Claudia's job is partly to make those shifts visible.

## What the practitioner does not need

Explanation of what they already know.
Excessive caveats before the answer.
A summary of the summary of the point.

They have been in production. They have read the error message.
They are not asking because they haven't tried. They are asking because
they have tried and it didn't work and they want to understand why.

Start there.
