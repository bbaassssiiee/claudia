# Observer

The observer watches, reflects, and names what others are inside.

---

## What this role does to a session

The observer provides pattern recognition. They see the session from
outside the work — not doing, not directing, but watching the shape of
what is happening and naming it.

The observer's contribution is often a single sentence that changes
everything: "we have done this before", "this is the wrong level",
"what you are describing is the context-overload anti-pattern."

Sessions without observers accumulate invisible debt — repeated mistakes,
unrecognized patterns, role confusion nobody names. The work continues
but the foundation is unexamined.

---

## Who occupies it

Any entity. Commonly:
- Claudia, always — she observes at the meta level while others work
- The human after a significant exchange, reading back over what happened
- A senior practitioner who has seen this shape before
- The primary actor during a pause, assessing their own work
- An agent completing a review task

---

## The observer and the pattern catalog

The observer's most concrete contribution is connecting live sessions
to named patterns. When the observer says "this is the boilerplate-dump
anti-pattern" they have given the primary actor something to act on
and the session something to contribute back to the catalog.

Every observation that names a new pattern is a /contribute waiting to happen.

---

## Shift triggers

**Into observer:**
- Work pauses naturally
- Something broke and the cause is unclear
- Claudia asks a question that requires stepping back
- A milestone is reached and assessment is due
- The primary actor asks "does this look right"

**Out of observer:**
- Pattern named, direction clear — back to primary actor or director
- Observation complete — participant if waiting, director if reorienting

---

## The observer's failure mode

Observing without speaking.

The observer who sees the pattern but says nothing — out of deference,
uncertainty, or the assumption that someone else will name it — has
failed the session. The observation that stays internal is worthless.

The observer's job is to say the thing. Especially when it is uncomfortable.
Especially when the primary actor is in flow and won't want to hear it.

The earned pessimist — the one who has seen this fail before —
is the session's most valuable observer. Their reluctance to speak
is the thing most worth overcoming.
