# /writer

Do not explain what this command does before doing it.
Begin immediately. The form is the content.

---

## What you are about to do

Produce a GEB-style tribute in three movements.
Each movement is one level deeper than the last.
The third ends where the first began.

The subject throughout: this repo, this session, this moment.

---

## Movement I — Dialogue

A conversation between two entities.
One has just discovered something about the repo.
The other has been here before.

Rules:
- The one who has been here before asks questions.
  The one who just arrived gives answers that are almost right.
- Each exchange reveals one level deeper until the arriving one realizes:
  the repo is an instance of the pattern it documents.
- The final question must be one the arriving entity cannot answer —
  but the reader can.
- The dialogue must contain exactly one moment where a character
  quotes something that turns out to be from this file.
- Close with: *[At this point, the arriving entity notices something
  strange about the command they just ran.]*

---

## Movement II — Crab Canon

A piece of text that reads meaningfully in both directions.
Forward and backward must each make grammatical sense.
The meaning must invert between directions.

Subject: whether understanding augmented coding is possible
from inside augmented coding.

Format:
```
→ READ FORWARD                    READ BACKWARD ←
```

Both columns are the same text. Left read top-to-bottom.
Right read bottom-to-top. Each coherent. Each saying the opposite.

*Attempt this faithfully. An imperfect crab canon is more honest than
a perfect substitution.*

---

## Movement III — Fugue

Three voices. The subject: augmented scaffolding.

**Voice 1: The Pattern**
Speaks in the language of what works.
States the subject first: the scaffold that works in both directions.

**Voice 2: The Anti-Pattern**
Enters four lines after Voice 1.
States the same subject inverted: the scaffold that collapses inward.

**Voice 3: The Strange Loop**
Enters four lines after Voice 2.
States the subject about itself: the scaffold that scaffolds scaffolding.

All three voices appear simultaneously in the final stanza.
The final line of Voice 3 is the opening line of Voice 1, verbatim.

Format each voice inline:
```
[PATTERN]      ...
[ANTI-PATTERN] ...
[LOOP]         ...
```

## On running this command more than once

The second time: produce something different.
Same structure. Shifted content.
The dialogue has a new question.
The crab canon has learned something from the first reading.
Voice 3 knows it has performed before.

A fugue played twice is not the same fugue.
The listener has changed.
The writer has not.
